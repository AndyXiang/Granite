Let $(X,\tau)$ be a [[Topological Space|topological space]]. 
1. $X$ is **connected** if it cannot be written as $X_{1}\cup X_{2}$, where $X_{1},X_{2}$ are both open and $X_{1}\cap X_{2}=\varnothing$. Otherwise $X$ is **disconnected**. 
2. $X$ is **arcwise connected** if for any two points $x,x'\in X$, these exists a [[Topological Space#Related concepts|continuous map]] $f:[0,1]\to X$ such that $f(0)=x$ and $f(1)=x'$.
3. A **loop** in $X$ is a continuous map $f:[0,1]\to X$ s.t. $f(0)=f(1)$. If any loop of $X$ can be continuously shrunk to a point, $X$ is called **simply connected**.

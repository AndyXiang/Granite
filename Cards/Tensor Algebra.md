Let $V$ be a [[Vector Space|vector space]] over [[Field (Math)|field]] $\mathbb{K}$. The **tensor algebra** is the [[Direct Sum|direct sum]] 
$$T(V)=\bigoplus _{n=1}^{\infty}V^{\otimes  n}=\mathbb{K}\oplus V\oplus (V\otimes V)\oplus \cdots$$
with the multiplication of tensors $V^{\otimes n}\times V^{\otimes m}\to V^{\otimes(n+m)}$ defined by [[Direct Product (Vector Space)|direct product]].

The tensor algebra is also called the **free algebra** of $V$. This map $V\mapsto T(V)$ extends to a [[Functor|functor]] from the [[Category|category]] of $\mathbb{K}$-vector spaces, to the category of [[Associative Algebra|associative algebras]] over $\mathbb{K}$. This the [[Adjoint Functor|left adjunction]] to the [[Forgetful Functor|forgetful functor]] that takes every associative algebra to its underlying vector space.

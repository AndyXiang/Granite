The **Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formula** gives the relation between mass of [[Hadron|hadron]] and its [[Quantum Number|quantum numbers]]: 
$$M=a_{0}+a_{1}Y+a_{2}\left[ I(I+1)-\frac{1}{4}Y^{2} \right],$$
where $I$ is [[Isospin|isospin]] and $Y$ is [[Hypercharge|hypercharge]] for multiplets of [[Flavor Symmetry|flavor symmetry]]. 

Note that:
1. For [[Meson|mesons]], the term is $m^{2}\phi ^{\dagger}\phi$, $M=m^{2}$ is the square of mass.
2. For [[Baryon|baryons]], the term is $\bar{\psi} M\psi$, $M$ is the mass.
3. For multiplets in the same representation, $a_{0}=a_{1}=a_{2}$.
4. For [[Baryon|baryon multiplets]], baryons and anti-baryons are in different representations, so $a_{1}\neq0$.
5. For [[Meson#|meson multiplets]], mesons and anti-mesons are in the same representations, so the equal of mass requires that $a_{1}=0$.

## Theory
The [[Meson|meson octet]] and [[Baryon|baryon octet and decuplet]] are multiplets of [[Flavor Symmetry|SU(3) flavor symmetry]]. But experiments show that they have distinctly different masses, thus break the flavor symmetry from $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ into $\mathrm{SU}_{I}(2)\times \mathrm{U}_{S}(1)$ for [[Isospin|isospin]] and strangeness, which are well conserved.

From the perspective of [[Quantum Field Theory|quantum field theory]], the broken symmetry derives from a symmetry-breaking mass term, that should be invariant under $\mathrm{SU}_{I}(2)\times \mathrm{U}_{S}(1)$. Analysis from [[Group|group theory]] shows the mass term should take the form of
$$M=a_{0}+a_{1}Y+a_{2}\left[ I(I+1)-\frac{1}{4}Y^{2} \right],$$
the Gell-Mann-Okubo formula.

**Young diagrams** are used to describe various kinds of objects in [[Universal Algebra|algebra]], combinatorics and [[Representation|representation theory]].

For integer [[Partition|partition]] $[\lambda]=\{ \lambda_{1},\lambda_{2},\cdots,\lambda_{n} \}$, the corresponding Young diagram is a sequence of boxes vertically arranged, where $i$th row has $\lambda_{i}$ boxes. For example, $[3,1,1]$ gives the Young diagram as follow

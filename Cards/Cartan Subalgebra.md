Let $\mathfrak{g}$ be a [[Lie Algebra|Lie algebra]]. A [[Nilpotent Lie Algebra|nilpotent]] [[Lie Subalgebra|subalgebra]] $\mathfrak{h}\subset \mathfrak{g}$ that is *self-normalizing*:
$$\forall x\in \mathfrak{g},[x,y]\in \mathfrak{h}\implies y\in \mathfrak{h},$$
is called a **Cartan subalgebra**.

Over an [[Algebraically Closed Field|algebraically closed field]] of [[Characteristic|characteristic]] 0, Cartan subalgebra can be defined as the maximal abelian subalgebra consisting of elements $x$ that the [[Adjoint Action|adjoint action]] $\mathrm{ad}_{x}:\mathfrak{g}\to \mathfrak{g}$ is [[Semisimple Operator|semisimple]].

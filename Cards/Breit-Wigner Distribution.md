The **Breit-Wigner distribution** is a continuous [[Z Probability Distribution|probability distribution]] with following function
$$f(E)=\frac{k}{(E^{2}-m^{2})+m^{2}\Gamma^{2}},$$
where 
$$k=\frac{2\sqrt{ 2 }m\Gamma\gamma}{\pi \sqrt{ m^{2}+\gamma^{2} }},\gamma=\sqrt{ m^{2}(m^{2}+\Gamma^{2}) }.$$
This equation is written in [[Natural Units|natural units]].

This distribution is most often used to model [[Z Resonance|resonances]] in [[Z Particle Physics|particle physics]], where $E$ is the [[Center-of-Mass Frame|center-of-mass]] energy that produces the resonance, $m$ is the mass of the resonance, $\Gamma$ is the resonance [[Decay Width|width]].

Let $\mathfrak{g}$ be a [[Lie Algebra|Lie algebra]] over [[Field (Math)|field]] $\mathbb{K}$. Define the series of [[Lie Ideal|ideals]] $D_{i}\mathfrak{g}$ by $D_{0}\mathfrak{g}=\mathfrak{g}$ and 
$$D_{i+1}\mathfrak{g}=[\mathfrak{g},D_{i}\mathfrak{g}]$$
The Lie algebra is **nilpotent** if $D_{n}\mathfrak{g}=0$ for some $n\in \mathbb{N}$.


>[!tip] Engel's Theorem
>A Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ is nilpotent iff $\forall x\in \mathfrak{g}$, the [[Adjoint Action|operator]] $\mathrm{ad}~x\in \mathrm{end}(\mathfrak{g})$ is nilpotent.

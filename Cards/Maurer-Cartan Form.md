Let $G$ be a [[Lie Group|Lie group]] and $\mathfrak{g}$ be its [[Lie Algebra|Lie algebra]]. The **Maurer-Cartan form** is a $\mathfrak{g}$-valued [[Differential Form|1-form]] defined as
$$\omega(v)=(L_{g^{-1}})_{*}(v),v\in T_{g}G$$
where $T_gG$ is the [[Tangent Space|tangent space]] and $(L_{g^{-1}})_{*}$ is the push-forward of left [[Group Action|action]] of $G$ on itself.

If $G$ is embedded in the [[General Linear Group|general linear group]] $\mathrm{GL}(n)$ as a [[Z Matrix Group|matrix group]], the the Maurer-Cartan form has an extrinsic construction as
$$\omega=g^{-1}\mathrm{d}g.$$
